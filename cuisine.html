Food & Cuisine

A trip to Davao del Sur is a feast for the senses. Indulge in the "King of Fruits," the Durian, along with sweet pomelos, marang, and mangosteens. Don't leave without trying Sinuglaw (a mix of grilled pork and fish ceviche), eating fresh seafood by the gulf, or sipping a hot cup of locally brewed Arabica coffee from the slopes of Mt. Apo.

Fresh Seafood

Devour the daily catch straight from the Davao Gulf, grilled to perfection.

Exotic Fruits

Taste the "King of Fruits" alongside the sweetest pomelos and mangosteens.

Highland Brews

Sip award-winning, single-origin Arabica coffee grown in the volcanic soil of Mt. Apo.

A Taste of the South

The culinary landscape of Davao del Sur is a vibrant mix of coastal harvests and rich agricultural bounty. Thanks to its incredibly fertile volcanic soil, the province produces world-class tropical fruits and some of the best coffee beans in the Philippines. You cannot visit without exploring the local sutukil eateries (Sugba-Tuwa-Kilaw), where you handpick fresh seafood to be grilled, made into a rich soup, or cured in vinegar. Don't leave without trying Sinuglaw, a mouth-watering combination of grilled pork belly and raw fish ceviche that perfectly captures the bold flavors of Mindanao.

Must-Try Flavors

The Sinuglaw Experience

A perfect explosion of smoky, sour, spicy, and savory flavors in a single iconic dish.

Durian & Marang

Dive into the rich, custard-like textures of the region's most famous and polarizing tropical fruits.

Mt. Apo Coffee

Visit a local cafe to try the smooth, chocolatey notes of authentic Kapatagan Arabica.

Travel Tips

Getting There

Fly to Francisco Bangoy International Airport in Davao City, then take a bus south.

Best Time to Visit

November to May is the dry season — ideal for outdoor activities and beach trips.

Budget Friendly

Davao del Sur offers affordable accommodations, food, and transport options.
