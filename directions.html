How to Get There

Reaching Davao del Sur is easy and accessible. The primary entry point is the Francisco Bangoy International Airport in neighboring Davao City, which caters to domestic and international flights. From the airport or Davao City proper, you can take an air-conditioned bus or a passenger van heading south. The scenic drive to the provincial capital, Digos City, takes roughly 1.5 to 2 hours.

Fly to Davao City

Arrive via Francisco Bangoy International Airport, the main gateway to the region.

Smooth Land Travel

Enjoy a scenic, fully-paved highway drive down south from the airport.

Local Transport

Easily hop between towns using buses, passenger vans, or colorful local tricycles.

Navigating Your Way South

Getting to Davao del Sur is straightforward and accessible. The province is highly connected to neighboring regions by a network of well-maintained national highways, making it an easy trip for backpackers and road-trippers alike. Most travelers fly into Davao City. From there, head to the Ecoland Bus Terminal where air-conditioned buses bound for Digos City depart every 30 minutes. The trip takes about 1.5 to 2 hours. Alternatively, you can hire a private van at the airport for a more comfortable and direct journey straight to your resort.

Travel Options

Ecoland Bus Terminal

Catch a premium, air-conditioned bus heading south. Look for signs pointing to Digos or Cotabato.

Van Terminals (UV Express)

Navigate a surreal, moon-like landscape of giant, stark white volcanic rocks and active, steaming sulfur vents near the peak.

Renting a Car

Rent a private vehicle or a 4x4 in Davao City for ultimate freedom in exploring the highlands.

Travel Tips

Getting There

Fly to Francisco Bangoy International Airport in Davao City, then take a bus south.

Best Time to Visit

November to May is the dry season — ideal for outdoor activities and beach trips.

Budget Friendly

Davao del Sur offers affordable accommodations, food, and transport options.
